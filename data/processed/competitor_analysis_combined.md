# Competitor Ad Analysis — Combined Summary
*2026-06-11*

## Patterns common across all competitors

All three competitors lean heavily on **simplicity and speed as core value propositions**, using repetitive, action-oriented CTAs like "Install now," "Download," and "Learn more." Video is the dominant format for long-running campaigns, with carousels as a secondary workhorse. Each runs evergreen creative for 170+ days, indicating confidence in messaging consistency over novelty. Notably, **crypto is either framed as simple commodity access (Coinbase) or completely absent** (Cash App, Strike's data set). None lead with security, regulatory compliance, or network superiority—trust is assumed or implied through app store ratings and longevity, not technical differentiation.

## What makes each competitor distinct

**Coinbase** uses formulaic, multi-coin accessibility messaging ("Makes [Coin] Easy") to position itself as the general-purpose on-ramp to crypto. It avoids fee discussions entirely and leans on anti-establishment framing ("broken financial system") to attract ideological buyers, but its tone is transactional—download, buy, done. No education, no use-case evangelism beyond "you should own this."

**Cash App** owns the **zero-fee money utility** lane, promoting instant transfers, cash back, and no-credit-check loans. Crypto is invisible in their current ad strategy; they've chosen to compete on everyday financial friction removal (budgeting, rewards, BNPL) rather than speculative asset acquisition. Their carousel-heavy approach showcases breadth of use cases, not depth in any one category.

**Strike's data set** appears to be misattributed (gaming/e-commerce ads unrelated to Lightning payments), but if this reflects their actual ad presence, it suggests **zero consumer-facing advertising for Lightning wallets at scale**—a vacuum Speed can exploit.

## The biggest gap Speed can exploit

**The "instant global money for real life" positioning is completely unclaimed.** Coinbase sells coins as investments, Cash App sells fee-free dollars for domestic errands, and Strike (if data is accurate) isn't visibly competing in consumer messaging at all. None connect Lightning's **speed + borderless utility** to tangible, everyday use cases like remittance, tipping creators, iGaming payouts, or merchant payments.

Speed should own **"money that moves like the internet"**—positioning Lightning not as crypto-for-crypto's-sake, but as the **infrastructure for instant, zero-fee global transactions** that traditional rails (Venmo, Western Union, bank wires) can't match. Frame Speed Stacks as the reward for living in the new financial internet, target remittance corridors and crypto-curious gamers with Lightning's speed advantage, and use fee transparency (not avoidance, but **structural elimination via Lightning**) as proof of technological superiority, not just a promotional gimmick. No competitor is selling Lightning's **functional magic**—they're selling either speculative simplicity or domestic convenience. Speed can own the category between them.

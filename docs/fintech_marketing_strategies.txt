1. DIASPORA / REMITTANCE COMMUNITY MARKETING
==========================================================================

The remittance corridor is won at the community level, not the billboard level. Trust travels through relationships, not ads.

TACTIC 1 - CORRIDOR-SPECIFIC WHATSAPP GROUP SEEDING
Identify existing US-Mexico and US-Brazil WhatsApp groups organized around hometown associations (e.g., Zacatecas clubs in LA, Minas Gerais networks in Boston). Partner with one trusted admin per group to share a genuine fee-comparison demonstration, not a pitch. Send a real $50 transfer on video and show the recipient getting it in seconds with zero fees.
Why it works: peer witness carries ten times the credibility of a paid ad in high-trust community spaces.
Guardrail/risk: the admin partnership is a paid or gifted relationship and must be disclosed per FTC guidelines; never imply the demo is spontaneous if it was arranged.

TACTIC 2 - HOMETOWN ASSOCIATION EVENT SPONSORSHIP
Sponsor the annual dinner or soccer tournament of federaciones (Mexican hometown federations) in Chicago, Dallas, Houston. Provide branded items, offer a live transfer demo table, and donate a small amount in sats to the association's home-project fund in Mexico.
Why it works: tangible community investment earns organic word-of-mouth in a corridor where referrals drive almost all switching behavior.
Guardrail/risk: confirm Speed holds the required money-transmitter licenses in the relevant US states before activating; do not activate in unlicensed states.

TACTIC 3 - CHURCH AND TITHE-COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIPS
Approach Spanish-language evangelical and Catholic parishes in remittance-heavy zip codes. Offer a free "send money home" workshop after Sunday service, co-hosted by the parish treasurer. No hard sell; just a demonstration and a QR code.
Why it works: financial advice from a trusted faith institution dramatically lowers adoption friction for unbanked or underbanked migrants.
Guardrail/risk: avoid any implication of church endorsement in paid materials; keep language clearly educational.

TACTIC 4 - MICRO-CREATORS IN THE RECEIVING COUNTRY
Pay creators in Guadalajara or Belo Horizonte to document receiving a Speed transfer, showing the pesos or reais arriving instantly on screen. The receiving-end story is almost never told in remittance marketing.
Why it works: the recipient's family is the emotional trigger for the sender's decision; showing their experience closes the loop.
Guardrail/risk: Brazil and Mexico have specific rules on cross-border financial promotions; disclosures must appear in the local language, and claims must be accurate at time of publication.

2. IGAMING AFFILIATE MODELS
==========================================================================

Lightning is structurally superior for iGaming deposits, and the affiliate ecosystem already exists at scale. Speed should plug into it, not rebuild it.

TACTIC 1 - CPA DEALS WITH LICENSED CASINO AFFILIATES
Negotiate cost-per-acquisition deals with established affiliate networks (Income Access, Catena Media, Better Collective) where Speed pays a flat fee for each verified depositing user referred. Structure the offer around Speed's instant-deposit angle, not on gambling outcomes.
Why it works: iGaming affiliates have massive, pre-qualified audiences of deposit-motivated users; Speed gets warm traffic.
Guardrail/risk: Speed must only work with affiliates promoting licensed operators in jurisdictions where Speed is authorized to operate; UK/Malta affiliates require ASA-compliant disclosures and must exclude self-excluded players via affiliate agreement language.

TACTIC 2 - PAYMENT-METHOD CONTENT ON AFFILIATE REVIEW SITES
Reach out to casino-review sites to add a "Lightning deposit" category to their payment-method comparison tables. Provide accurate, factual copy they can use.
Why it works: payment method is a top-three filter for crypto-comfortable iGaming users; ranking in those tables drives high-intent organic referrals.
Guardrail/risk: content must be factually accurate; any sponsored placement must be labeled; avoid jurisdictions where crypto gambling advertising is prohibited (e.g., UK requires licensed operator context, not just a wallet).

TACTIC 3 - OPERATOR PARTNERSHIP WITH BUILT-IN PLAYER INCENTIVE
Negotiate directly with licensed operators to offer a Speed-exclusive deposit bonus (e.g., 10% extra on first Lightning deposit). Speed co-funds the bonus as a customer-acquisition cost.
Why it works: operator-backed incentives convert at the moment of deposit intent, the highest-value moment in the funnel.
Guardrail/risk: bonus advertising must comply with UK CAP/BCAP codes or equivalent; terms must be clear, wagering requirements disclosed, and the offer unavailable to self-excluded or underage users.

Sponsor existing Bitcoin meetups in Miami, Austin, Berlin, and Prague with a small cash contribution for venue costs. Send a team member who can do a live merchant-payment or remittance demo on stage, not a slide deck.

Guardrail/risk: avoid any price-appreciation language or investment framing; keep all claims operational.

TACTIC 2 - MERCHANT ADOPTION MAPS
Build and publicly maintain a map of merchants accepting Speed payments in target cities. Actively recruit five to ten anchor merchants per city with a three-month zero-fee processing guarantee.
Why it works: a visible merchant network creates a use-case feedback loop that attracts users and press simultaneously.
Guardrail/risk: ensure merchant agreements are clear on settlement terms and that Speed's money-transmission licensing covers merchant processing in each state.

TACTIC 3 - SATS-BACK DEVELOPER BOUNTIES
Offer small sats-denominated bounties for open-source integrations, WooCommerce plugins, or LNURL tools that connect to Speed. Post bounties on Stacker News and GitHub.
Why it works: developers who build on Speed become its most credible public advocates, and the integrations compound in value.
Guardrail/risk: bounty payouts may constitute taxable income; provide recipients with necessary documentation and ensure Speed's legal team reviews any open-source licensing implications.

TACTIC 4 - ORANGE-PILLING CONTENT WITH HONEST FRAMING
Produce short-form educational content showing specific problems Speed solves (remittance fees, slow settlement) rather than Bitcoin price or generalized freedom narratives. Distribute on YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels with Spanish and Portuguese subtitles.
Why it works: problem-first framing converts crypto-curious users who are skeptical of hype but motivated by concrete savings.
Guardrail/risk: never imply sats will appreciate; all savings claims must be verified and based on current actual fee comparisons.

4. FINTECH REFERRAL MECHANICS THAT WORK
==========================================================================

TACTIC 1 - DOUBLE-SIDED MILESTONE REFERRAL
Give both referrer and referred user a reward only after the referred user completes a qualifying action (e.g., sends first $20 transfer). Reward in sats, not cash, to reduce regulatory complexity.
Why it works: milestone gating eliminates fake-account fraud and aligns the incentive with genuine activation, not just sign-up.
Guardrail/risk: sats rewards that have monetary value may still trigger money-transmission or securities considerations depending on structure; get legal review before launch.

TACTIC 2 - COMMUNITY AMBASSADOR TIERS
Create a three-tier ambassador program for top referrers in diaspora communities. Higher tiers get exclusive perks (early features, Speed-branded merchandise, invitations to product calls) rather than purely cash escalation.
Why it works: non-cash status rewards create sustained advocacy without triggering runaway payout liability or regulatory scrutiny around unlicensed money distribution.
Guardrail/risk: any ambassador who earns above a threshold value in rewards must receive a 1099 (US) or equivalent; build reporting infrastructure before scale.

TACTIC 3 - FAMILY-CHAIN REFERRAL FOR REMITTANCE CORRIDORS
When a user sends their first transfer, prompt them to invite the recipient to also download Speed. The recipient, once activated, gets a small sats credit on their first send. This creates a bidirectional corridor relationship.
Why it works: the recipient already has demonstrated trust in the product the moment they receive funds; conversion rate at that moment is dramatically higher than cold acquisition.
Guardrail/risk: the recipient incentive is a promotional offer and must be disclosed; ensure the cross-border payment of rewards does not violate local regulations in Mexico or Brazil.

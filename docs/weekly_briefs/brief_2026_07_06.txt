This week's data reveals three critical findings. First, Apple Search Ads is delivering the lowest acquisition cost across all paid channels at $2.40 eCPI for the US Brand Exact campaign, nearly half the cost of Google Ads brand campaigns despite targeting the same intent. Second, organic installs dominate the funnel at 28,467 installations with zero paid spend, representing 66% of total volume and signaling strong product-market fit or effective unpaid distribution. Third, Meta's Cash Deposit campaign is bleeding budget at an $80.57 eCPI with only 16 installs from $1,289 in spend, a failure rate that demands immediate action.

Apple Search Ads is the most efficient paid channel right now, clocking a blended $3.13 eCPI across 3,063 installs. The US Brand Exact campaign alone hit $2.40 eCPI while converting at scale through 1,889 installs. This performance advantage stems from high-intent search behavior and Apple's tighter audience targeting in app store environments. Users actively searching for wallet or Lightning payment solutions are closer to conversion, and Apple's lower inventory competition compared to Google's display network keeps bid costs contained. The channel is under-indexed relative to its efficiency, currently representing just 7% of total installs despite offering the best unit economics.

Meta advertising needs immediate budget review and reallocation. While the Payday Android Broad+ campaign performs well at $3.00 eCPI with strong 7.58% click-through rates, the Cash Deposit creative is catastrophically inefficient at $80.57 per install despite attracting clicks. The retargeting and re-engagement campaigns are similarly troubled, with eCPIs of $15.59 and $24.07 respectively, suggesting creative fatigue or misaligned audience definitions. The platform's blended $4.17 eCPI masks severe performance divergence within the Meta suite.

Kill the Cash Deposit campaign immediately and reallocate its remaining budget to Apple Search Ads brand campaigns. The Cash Deposit creative has spent $1,289 to acquire just 16 users, a cost structure that cannot support any reasonable LTV scenario even in high-value iGaming segments. Meanwhile, Apple's Brand Exact campaign demonstrates it can acquire users at $2.40 while maintaining volume in the four-figure install range. Shifting $1,000–$1,500 monthly from underperforming Meta campaigns to Apple could yield 400–600 additional installs at sustainable economics, directly improving blended CAC across the paid portfolio.

D1 retention is flat, averaging 26.8% over the last seven matured cohorts compared to 26.3% the prior week, a statistically negligible 0.4 percentage point gain. The metric oscillates between 23% and 30% without establishing a clear directional trend, suggesting current acquisition channels are delivering consistent user quality but the onboarding experience has plateaued. Flat retention at this volume signals stability but not growth; without intervention in first-session activation or value delivery, CAC improvements will drive profitability but won't unlock viral or compounding retention curves. The 29.9% result on July 2 offers a data point worth investigating for campaign or creative differences that day.

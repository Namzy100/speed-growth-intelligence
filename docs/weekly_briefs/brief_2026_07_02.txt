This week's data reveals three critical findings. First, organic channels now drive 66 percent of all installs—28,015 from the Organic channel alone plus 1,068 from Google Organic Search and 1,709 from Website—representing a strong and free acquisition engine that dwarfs paid volume. Second, paid acquisition performance is bifurcating sharply: Facebook's flagship Payday campaign delivers installs at $3.23 while Google Ads campaigns hover near $4.90, a 52 percent cost differential that demands immediate scrutiny. Third, within Meta's portfolio, the Cash Deposit campaign burned $1,113 for just 13 installs at $85.63 per install, a catastrophic efficiency failure that suggests either severe targeting misalignment or creative-to-landing-page breakdown.

Facebook is the most efficient paid channel right now, driven entirely by the Payday Android Broad+ campaign which posted a $3.11 eCPI and a robust 7.82 percent click-through rate. This campaign accounts for 1,607 of Facebook's 1,638 installs and demonstrates clear product-market fit with Speed's remittance segment, likely resonating with users who experience cash-flow pressure around payday and need instant, zero-fee transfers. The broad targeting appears to be surfacing high-intent users without the efficiency penalty typically associated with wide audiences, and the creative is clearly punching through the noise.

Google Ads urgently needs a budget review. The channel's blended eCPI of $4.85 is 42 percent higher than Facebook's, and even the best-performing Google campaign—New Android Brand US at $4.86—costs more than Facebook's worst. With 5,962 installs at over $28,000 in spend, Google Ads consumed the lion's share of paid budget but delivered materially worse unit economics. The XAUT offers campaign, which pulled 1,853 installs at $4.92, may be targeting crypto-curious users with a product hook that's too niche or confusing, driving clicks but not conversions.

The recommended action is to immediately pause the Meta Cash Deposit campaign and reallocate its budget to scaling the Payday Android Broad+ campaign. The Cash Deposit creative generated 147,191 impressions and a respectable 3,489 clicks, proving it can capture attention, but the 13-install outcome signals a catastrophic post-click experience—either the landing promise doesn't match the ad, the deposit flow is broken, or the offer isn't compelling to the targeted cohort. Rather than iterate blindly, shift that spend to the proven Payday winner and monitor whether efficiency holds at higher scale. If Payday saturates, test a Payday iOS variant before returning to deposit messaging.

D1 retention is improving, with the most recent seven-day average of matured cohorts at 26.6 percent compared to 25.2 percent the prior week, a 1.4 percentage-point gain. This upward trend suggests that recent product changes, onboarding tweaks, or shifts in traffic mix are attracting users with stronger intent or delivering a better first-session experience. The consistency of D7 retention in the 12 to 16 percent range across the past month reinforces that the gains are real, not noise, and that Speed is retaining a meaningful share of day-one survivors through the critical first week.

In the competitive landscape, Robinhood is emphasizing premium membership perks like Gold APY, advanced trading tools, and prediction markets with transparent pricing, targeting active traders rather than beginners and treating crypto as one asset class among many. Crypto.com leans heavily on gamification—missions, mystery boxes, and rewards—alongside card cashback and zero-fee promotions, prioritizing incentive-driven engagement over pure crypto utility.

For EU expansion, Germany should be Speed's first entry point due to 1,657 organic installs signaling untapped demand, a large crypto-curious segment, and the regulatory advantage of operating within the EU's single EMI license framework. The United Kingdom ranks second with the strongest iGaming market in Europe and the highest-volume UK-to-India and UK-to-Nigeria remittance corridors, though it requires separate post-Brexit regulatory work. Portugal comes third for its unmatched Brazil corridor concentration on a per-capita basis, offering a dense, word-of-mouth-friendly launch among Brazilian nationals before broader scaling.

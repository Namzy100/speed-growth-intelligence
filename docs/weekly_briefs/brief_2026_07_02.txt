This week delivered three standout findings. First, organic installs now represent 66% of total volume at 28,015 installs, a massive signal of word-of-mouth traction or brand momentum that deserves deeper attribution analysis to understand which segments and geographies are driving unprompted downloads. Second, Apple Search Ads is running at an eCPI of $2.69 for the brand exact campaign, nearly half the cost of Google Ads brand at $4.86, making it the clear efficiency leader among paid channels. Third, the Meta Cash Deposit campaign is burning budget at an eCPI of $85.63 with only 13 installs from $1,113 in spend, a glaring misfire that needs immediate review or pause.

Apple Search Ads is the most efficient channel right now, delivering 2,753 installs at a blended $3.44 eCPI with the brand exact campaign performing exceptionally at $2.69. The channel is capturing high-intent users already searching for Speed Wallet by name, and the conversion rate from impressions to installs is strong. This suggests brand awareness is building and users are actively seeking the app, making every dollar spent on Apple highly effective compared to the $4.85 eCPI on Google Ads.

Meta needs immediate budget attention. While the Payday Android Broad campaign is performing well at $3.11 eCPI and driving 1,607 installs, the Cash Deposit campaign is a catastrophic outlier at $85.63 per install. The retargeting and re-engagement campaigns are also underperforming at $17.23 and $24.07 eCPI respectively, suggesting either audience fatigue, poor creative fit, or misaligned targeting. The total Meta spend of $7,089 yielded a blended $4.16 eCPI, but stripping out the Payday campaign reveals the rest of the Meta portfolio is wildly inefficient.

Pause the Cash Deposit campaign immediately and reallocate that budget to the Payday Android Broad campaign, which is proven, scalable, and delivering installs at less than one-tenth the cost. The Payday creative and targeting are clearly resonating with the remittance and crypto-curious segments, and scaling it will drive volume without sacrificing efficiency. Use the freed budget to test a new video variant within the Payday framework rather than trying to salvage Cash Deposit.

D1 retention is improving, up 1.4 percentage points from 25.2% to 26.6% over the past two weeks of matured cohorts. This signals that recent user quality is strengthening, likely driven by the efficient Apple and Google brand campaigns pulling in higher-intent users compared to broader prospecting. The trend is encouraging and suggests onboarding or product changes made in early June are beginning to show measurable impact on day-one engagement.

Robinhood is doubling down on premium features and advanced trading tools, emphasizing Gold membership perks, low margin rates, and professional-grade platforms rather than beginner simplicity. Crypto.com is leaning heavily into gamification and rewards, pushing missions, mystery boxes, card cashback, and zero-fee promotions to drive engagement. Both are targeting active traders and reward-motivated users, leaving room for Speed to own the simplicity and zero-fee remittance lanes with less competitive noise.

Germany should be Speed's first EU market, driven by 1,657 existing organic installs that represent untapped demand and a strong crypto-curious base, plus access to the Indian remittance corridor under a single EMI license. The UK ranks second due to the highest-volume India and Nigeria remittance corridors and the most developed iGaming market in Europe, though it requires separate UK regulatory work. Portugal takes third for its dense Brazil corridor concentration, offering a focused, word-of-mouth-driven launch opportunity among Brazilian nationals before broader scaling.

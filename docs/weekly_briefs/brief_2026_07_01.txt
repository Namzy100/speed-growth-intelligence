This week's data reveals three notable patterns. First, Organic continues to dominate total volume with 28,015 installs representing 66% of all acquisition, signaling strong word-of-mouth and product-market fit but also highlighting our reliance on unpaid channels. Second, our paid acquisition landscape shows stark efficiency differences, with Apple Search Ads delivering installs at $2.69 to $4.89 while Meta's Cash Deposit and Re-targeting campaigns are burning budget at $85.63 and $17.23 per install respectively. Third, Meta's Payday Android Broad+ campaign is a clear outlier winner, driving 1,607 installs at just $3.11 eCPI with a 7.82% click-through rate, proving the remittance segment hook resonates when paired with broad targeting on Android.

Facebook is currently our most efficient paid channel at a blended $3.41 eCPI, but that figure masks the real story. The Payday Android Broad+ creative is doing the heavy lifting at $3.11 per install and accounts for 95% of Facebook's total installs. This campaign's combination of zero-fee remittance messaging, broad Android targeting, and strong creative execution is delivering near-Apple efficiency at scale. The 7.82% CTR suggests the value proposition is landing cleanly with remittance senders, and the volume proves this audience is deep enough to sustain spend.

Google Ads needs immediate budget review. With 5,962 installs at a blended $4.85 eCPI, it's consuming the lion's share of paid spend while delivering the highest cost per install among major channels. The New Android Brand US and XAUT offers campaigns are both hovering near $4.90 eCPI, nearly 60% more expensive than our best-performing Meta campaign. Given that brand search typically converts at lower cost, this suggests either bid inflation, poor quality scores, or creative fatigue. We're paying premium rates for what should be high-intent traffic.

Reallocate $5,000 from Google Ads brand campaigns to scale the Meta Payday Android Broad+ campaign immediately. The Payday creative has proven unit economics at $3.11 eCPI with room to scale—it's only spent $4,991 of Meta's total budget and is still delivering efficient installs with strong engagement metrics. Meanwhile, our Google brand campaigns are operating 57% above this benchmark despite targeting users already searching for Speed Wallet by name. This reallocation will lower blended acquisition cost while testing whether the remittance hook can maintain efficiency at higher volume before we commit to a full channel shift.

D1 retention is effectively flat, with the recent seven-day average at 26.4% compared to 25.5% the prior week, representing less than one percentage point of movement. The data shows natural daily variance between 23% and 31%, with no sustained trend in either direction. This stability suggests our current user mix and onboarding experience are consistent, but the flat trend also means we're not yet seeing retention improvements from product or messaging optimizations. The 26% baseline is workable but leaves significant room for improvement, particularly if we want to justify scaling paid spend beyond current levels.

Robinhood and Crypto.com are both leaning into sophistication and rewards rather than simplicity. Robinhood is emphasizing Gold membership APY, prediction markets, and advanced trading tools like their Legend platform, positioning crypto as one asset class among many for active traders. Crypto.com is running long campaigns around gamified rewards, missions, mystery boxes, card cashback, and zero-fee promotions, using incentive structures rather than ease-of-use to drive engagement.

Germany, the United Kingdom, and Portugal represent Speed's strongest EU entry sequence. Germany's 1,657 organic installs are the loudest demand signal in Europe and represent users we haven't yet monetized, while a single EMI license unlocks both Germany and future EU markets under one regulatory umbrella. The UK follows due to its established India and Nigeria remittance corridors and mature iGaming market, though it requires separate post-Brexit licensing. Portugal offers concentrated Brazil-corridor density on a per-capita basis, ideal for a focused launch that leverages word-of-mouth among Brazilian nationals before broader scaling.

This week's data reveals three critical findings. First, Facebook has quietly become the most cost-efficient paid channel at $3.23 per install for the Payday campaign, undercutting both Google Ads and Apple Search Ads by more than a dollar. Second, organic installs dominate with 28,015 of 42,412 total installs, signaling strong word-of-mouth or earned discovery—a powerful validation of product-market fit that paid channels should amplify rather than replace. Third, the Meta Cash Deposit campaign is hemorrhaging money at $85.63 per install with only 13 conversions from $1,113 in spend, a clear misfire that requires immediate intervention.

Facebook is the efficiency leader right now, delivering installs at $3.23 through the Payday Android Broad+ campaign. This creative is hitting the remittance sender segment at the right moment—payday timing—with broad targeting that still maintains a 7.82 percent click-through rate and converts at scale with 1,607 installs. It's outperforming Google Ads brand campaigns by 34 percent on cost and crushing Apple's max conversion efforts by a third. The combination of creative relevance, audience timing, and platform efficiency makes it the smart place to lean in.

The Meta Cash Deposit campaign needs urgent budget review or immediate pause. At $85.63 per install it's running 27 times more expensive than the best-performing Meta campaign and burning through budget with a 2.37 percent CTR that suggests weak creative-to-audience fit. With only 13 installs from over 147,000 impressions, this is either targeting the wrong segment or using messaging that doesn't resonate with Speed's iGaming hook. Every dollar here is an opportunity cost against scaling the Payday campaign.

The specific recommended action is to immediately pause the Cash Deposit Meta campaign, reallocate that budget to scale the Payday Android Broad+ campaign to $7,000 monthly spend, and test a payday-timed creative variant for iOS. The rationale is straightforward: Facebook has proven it can acquire users 60 percent cheaper than the blended paid average, the Payday hook is working, and we're leaving money on the table by not scaling the winner while a failed experiment continues to drain resources.

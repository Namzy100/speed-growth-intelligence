Outreach Brief — Coinbase (TikTok)
Segment: crypto-curious | Score: 55.4/100 | 78,300 followers
------------------------------------------------------------
Coinbase brings a crypto-curious audience of roughly 78K to the table, and while their content leans Bitcoin-heavy (around 27%), what stands out is the audience profile rather than raw reach. These are people interested in Bitcoin who haven't necessarily figured out what to actually *do* with it day to day. That gap is exactly where Speed fits — we're the wallet that turns "I own some Bitcoin" into "I use Bitcoin to pay for things." Their followers already trust them as an on-ramp, so positioning Speed as the next step (actually spending and earning, not just holding) feels natural rather than forced.

For this crypto-curious segment, the hook is simplicity and utility, not technical depth. Lead with how easy Speed makes everyday payments, the fact that it's available on iOS and Android for normal people, and Speed Stacks letting them earn sats on purchases they're already making. Stablecoin support is worth mentioning for the audience members nervous about volatility.

Three talking points to pitch: first, Speed bridges the awareness-to-action gap their audience sits in — they know Bitcoin, now they can use it. Second, instant, zero-fee Lightning payments make the "what's it good for" question answerable in one demo. Third, Speed Stacks gives a tangible reason to keep the app open beyond speculation.

Recommended CTA: invite them to a walkthrough demo and propose a short content piece showing a real purchase made with Speed, start to finish.

One thing to avoid: don't pitch this as a trading or investing play — their audience is curious, not deep in the weeds, and price-speculation framing undercuts the everyday-utility angle that makes Speed click.
